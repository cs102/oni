Here's a comprehensive overview of word families typically taught from Pre-K through 12th grade, organized by educational level:

## Pre-K to Kindergarten
**Short vowel families (CVC patterns):**
- -at: cat, bat, hat, mat, rat, sat
- -an: can, fan, man, pan, ran, tan
- -ap: cap, map, nap, tap, gap
- -et: bet, get, jet, let, met, net, pet, set, wet
- -en: den, hen, men, pen, ten
- -ig: big, dig, fig, pig, wig
- -in: bin, fin, pin, win, tin
- -ip: dip, hip, lip, rip, sip, tip, zip
- -ot: cot, dot, got, hot, lot, not, pot
- -op: cop, hop, mop, pop, top
- -ug: bug, dug, hug, jug, mug, rug, tug
- -un: bun, fun, run, sun

## 1st-2nd Grade
**Long vowel families:**
- -ake: bake, cake, make, take, wake, lake
- -ale: tale, male, pale, sale, whale
- -ame: came, game, name, same, tame
- -ane: cane, lane, mane, pane, plane
- -ate: date, gate, late, plate, skate
- -ice: dice, mice, nice, rice, slice, twice
- -ide: hide, ride, side, slide, wide
- -ine: dine, fine, line, mine, nine, pine, vine
- -ite: bite, kite, site, white
- -oke: joke, poke, woke, broke, choke, smoke
- -ope: hope, rope, slope
- -ose: hose, nose, rose, those
- -ute: cute, flute, mute

**Blends and digraphs:**
- -ack: back, pack, sack, track, black
- -ank: bank, tank, rank, thank
- -ash: cash, dash, flash, trash
- -ell: bell, sell, tell, shell, spell
- -ill: bill, fill, hill, will, spill
- -ock: dock, lock, rock, sock, block, clock
- -uck: duck, luck, truck, stuck

## 3rd-4th Grade
**Complex patterns:**
- -air: fair, hair, pair, chair, stair
- -are: care, dare, share, square
- -ear: bear, pear, wear, tear
- -ear: dear, fear, hear, near, year, clear
- -ight: bright, fight, light, night, right, sight, tight
- -ound: found, ground, round, sound, pound
- -own: brown, clown, crown, down, town
- -ow: blow, flow, grow, know, show, snow
- -oy: boy, joy, toy, enjoy
- -oil: boil, coil, soil, spoil
- -ool: cool, pool, school, tool
- -oon: moon, noon, soon, spoon
- -out: about, shout, sprout

## 5th-6th Grade
**Root word families (common prefixes/suffixes):**
- act: action, actor, active, react, enact, interact
- port: export, import, report, support, transport, portable
- dict: dictate, predict, contradict, dictionary
- struct: construct, instruct, structure, destroy
- form: conform, deform, inform, reform, transform
- ject: eject, inject, project, reject, subject
- tract: attract, contract, extract, subtract, distract

**Common suffix families:**
- -tion/-sion: nation, creation, division, decision
- -ful: beautiful, careful, helpful, peaceful, wonderful
- -less: careless, helpless, hopeless, useless
- -ness: darkness, happiness, kindness, sadness
- -ment: agreement, government, movement, payment

## 7th-8th Grade
**Latin/Greek roots:**
- bio (life): biology, biography, biodegradable, antibiotic
- geo (earth): geography, geology, geometry
- graph (write): autograph, photograph, paragraph, telegraph
- phon (sound): telephone, microphone, symphony, phonics
- scope (see): microscope, telescope, periscope
- tele (far): television, telephone, telegraph, teleport
- auto (self): automatic, automobile, autobiography, autopilot
- therm (heat): thermal, thermometer, thermostat
- meter (measure): diameter, perimeter, speedometer, thermometer

## 9th-10th Grade
**Advanced academic roots:**
- cred (believe): credit, credible, incredible, credential
- duc/duct (lead): conduct, educate, introduce, produce, reduce
- fac/fact (make): factory, manufacture, artifact, benefactor
- mitt/miss (send): admit, commit, dismiss, permit, submit, transmit
- rupt (break): corrupt, disrupt, erupt, interrupt, bankrupt
- scrib/script (write): describe, prescribe, manuscript, scripture
- spec/spect (look): inspect, respect, spectator, perspective, suspect
- vid/vis (see): video, visible, vision, supervise, evidence

## 11th-12th Grade
**Sophisticated academic vocabulary:**
- bene (good): benefit, benevolent, benefactor, beneficial
- mal (bad): malfunction, malicious, malnutrition, malpractice
- ambi (both): ambiguous, ambidextrous, ambivalent
- chron (time): chronic, chronological, synchronize, anachronism
- morph (form): metamorphosis, morphology, amorphous
- path (feeling): sympathy, empathy, apathy, pathetic, pathology
- phil (love): philosophy, philanthropy, Philadelphia
- dem (people): democracy, epidemic, demographic
- log/logue (word/study): dialogue, monologue, biology, logic
- meta (beyond): metaphor, metamorphosis, metabolism

This progression helps students build from simple phonetic patterns to complex morphological understanding, supporting both reading fluency and vocabulary development throughout their education.
